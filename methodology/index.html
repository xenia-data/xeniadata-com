How Xenia builds the data

A public overview. Full technical methodology is shared with qualifying partners under mutual non-disclosure agreement.

What this page is

This page describes our general approach to building the Xenia hotel dataset. It is intentionally non-specific about exact source registries, classification rules, scoring algorithms, conflict resolution heuristics, and tier-thresholding mechanics. That detail is part of our proprietary methodology and is shared only with API partners and prospective acquirers under written confidentiality.

Source approach

Xenia aggregates structured facts from a registry of public data sources. Sources are reviewed against permissive-use criteria before inclusion. We do not include data from sources whose terms prohibit our use, and we do not scrape paywalled or login-gated platforms. Source-by-source compliance is reviewed quarterly.

Fact-level provenance

Every fact in the Xenia dataset carries:

  • A reference to the source it was derived from
  • A confidence-tier classification (validated, probable, attested, or inference)
  • A confidence score for downstream filtering
  • An extraction timestamp

This provenance is exposed to partners through our query interface — they know exactly what they're consuming and can filter by source or tier as their use case requires.

Independent classification

Xenia classifies properties as independent, chain-affiliated, or branded-collection based on a multi-signal classifier. Chain affiliations and brand relationships are surfaced so partners can include or exclude as they see fit.

Conflict resolution

When multiple sources disagree about a fact, Xenia applies a multi-source resolution method that weighs source quality, recency, and corroboration count. The resolution and the underlying conflicting values are both retained in the dataset.

Right to respond

Listed hotels can claim, correct, supplement, or request removal of their Xenia profile at any time. We confirm authority to act on behalf of a property before processing changes. Response targets: 5 business days for factual correction; 30 days for full delisting.

What we don't do

  • We do not include verbatim review text from third-party platforms
  • We do not include defamatory or unverified negative-framed claims about individuals
  • We do not include personally identifiable information about guests or staff
  • We do not train a generative AI model on the source data — we use commercial LLMs as a generation tool downstream of our structured pipeline
